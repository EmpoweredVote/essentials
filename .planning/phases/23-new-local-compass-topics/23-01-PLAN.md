```sql
INSERT INTO inform.compass_stances
```

**Authoring rules for `description`, `supporting_points`, and `example_perspectives`:**

1. **Voice and style — model on existing LOCAL stances.** Read the three referenced stance seed files in this plan's <context>. Match their tone exactly: neutral, descriptive, third-person, mechanism-focused. The phrase "This stance holds that..." or "This stance supports..." or "This approach..." is a common opener — use varied versions of these openers.

2. **No national party framing — ever.** Do NOT use words like "progressive," "conservative," "liberal," "Democratic," "Republican," "left," "right," "MAGA," "woke." Describe positions by their local policy effect (who pays, who decides, what does the city do?), not by political affiliation. The existing LOCAL stances (Criminalization of Homelessness, Jail Capacity, Data Centers) deliberately avoid these labels — match that discipline.

3. **`description`** — 2-3 sentences (~3-5 lines of SQL after wrapping). Explain what the stance holds and the underlying reasoning. Reference the local policy mechanism (zoning votes, council budget, ICE detainer policy, etc.). DO escape any apostrophes as `''` (double single-quote) — e.g., "city's" becomes `'city''s'`.

4. **`supporting_points`** — exactly 3 items in the ARRAY. Each is a complete sentence stating one reason a person might hold this stance. Focus on substantive policy reasoning, NOT identity-based reasoning. Examples of good supporting points (from existing stances): "Neighborhood character and cohesion are built over decades and are difficult to restore once disrupted by rapid density changes." / "Democratic accountability: residents bear the consequences of zoning decisions and should have direct input through votes." Escape apostrophes as `''`.

5. **`example_perspectives`** — exactly 3 items in the ARRAY. Each follows the pattern "A [type of person] who [experience or belief] may see/view/find/value [this stance] as [reasoning]." Use varied subject types: homeowners, renters, parents, business owners, longtime residents, newcomers, advocates, professionals, etc. Avoid repeating the same archetype within a topic. Escape apostrophes as `''`.

6. **Center-stance neutrality (value 3)** — Position 3 is the moderate/balanced/middle-ground stance. Frame it as a thoughtful synthesis, not as evasive or wishy-washy. Existing examples (e.g., School Vouchers value 3) treat the middle position with the same weight as the extremes.

7. **Verbatim `text`** — The 50 `text` values must be copied EXACTLY from the research doc's "Proposed Question Texts" section. Do not edit, paraphrase, or "improve" them. These are user-approved labels.

**Critical constraints for Section 2:**
- Use the SELECT-FROM pattern above (NOT a DO $$ block — keep it consistent and simple).

```sql
INSERT INTO inform.compass_topic_roles (topic_id, role_scope, is_required)
SELECT t.id, 'local', true
FROM inform.compass_topics t
```

Use this CROSS JOIN pattern for the 4 LOCAL+STATE topics (Growth and Development Pace, Economic Development Incentives, Transportation Priorities, Rent Regulation):

```sql
SELECT t.id, scope.role_scope, true
FROM inform.compass_topics t
CROSS JOIN (VALUES ('local'), ('state')) AS scope(role_scope)
WHERE t.topic_key = '[topic_key]' AND t.is_live = true
ON CONFLICT (topic_id, role_scope) DO NOTHING;
```
