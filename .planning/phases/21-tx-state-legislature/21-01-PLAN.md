<mapping_resolution>
**RESOLVED ŌĆö DO NOT RE-VERIFY DURING EXECUTION**

The planner verified the MTFCC mapping by reading the live `essentialsService.ts` (lines 567-568), the official TIGER MTFCC spec, and Phase 21's own success criteria #1 in ROADMAP.md. All three sources agree:

- **G5210** = State Legislative District (Upper Chamber) = **Senate** ŌåÆ `STATE_UPPER`
- **G5220** = State Legislative District (Lower Chamber) = **House** ŌåÆ `STATE_LOWER`

This means the SLDU shapefile (Senate, 31 districts) gets `mtfcc='G5210'` and `district_type='STATE_UPPER'`. The SLDL shapefile (House, 150 districts) gets `mtfcc='G5220'` and `district_type='STATE_LOWER'`.

**Counterintuitive note:** The Census MTFCC text label for G5210 says "Upper Chamber" ŌĆö but the shapefile distributed at the SLDU URL is also "Upper Chamber" data. So G5210=SLDU=Upper. This matches the service code's join clause exactly.

**Why we do NOT clone load-ca-state-boundaries.ts:** That script's DISTRICTS array writes the inverted mapping (G5210ŌåÆSTATE_LOWER, G5220ŌåÆSTATE_UPPER), which contradicts both TIGER spec and the service code. CA's data is internally consistent under the buggy mapping (both columns flipped together) but breaks the service join. We deliberately follow `load-us-congressional-boundaries.ts` instead ŌĆö both for the auto-download pattern AND to avoid inheriting the inverted-mapping bug.
</mapping_resolution>
